  • About the Project

    This driver drowsiness detection project utilizes a combination of machine learning and computer vision techniques to enhance road safety by detecting signs of driver drowsiness. The project employs three distinct models:

    1. YOLOv3: YOLO (You Only Look Once) is a real-time object detection algorithm that detects various objects in images or video frames. In your project, YOLOv3 is utilized to detect and localize facial features and other relevant objects within the driver's field of view.

    2. VGG16: VGG16 is a deep convolutional neural network (CNN) architecture known for its simplicity and effectiveness in image classification tasks. In your project, the pre-trained VGG16 model is used to classify driver's eye states (e.g., open or closed) and detect yawning behavior, both of which are indicative of drowsiness.

    3. CNN (Custom): A custom CNN model trained from scratch is used to classify drowsiness based on various features extracted from the driver's facial expressions and movements. This model is tailored to the specific requirements of the drowsiness detection task and trained on a dataset comprising labeled examples of drowsy and alert states.

    The project aims to provide timely warnings or alerts to drivers or relevant authorities when signs of drowsiness are detected, thereby reducing the risk of accidents caused by driver fatigue or inattention. By leveraging multiple models, the system can capture a comprehensive range of indicators related to drowsiness, enhancing its accuracy and reliability in real-world scenarios.
